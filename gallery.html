Community Program in Pulau Carey

During my first year, I chose Bakti Siswa as my co-curricular subject. As part of the course, we planned and carried out a community outreach program in Pulau Carey, a place rich with cultural heritage and history. The program aimed to foster connections with the local community, particularly the indigenous Mah Meri people, known for their unique traditions and wooden mask carvings. Through various activities including educational sessions, cultural exchanges, and environmental clean-up efforts. We not only contributed to the community but also gained a deeper appreciation for their way of life. The experience was both eye-opening and humbling, strengthening our teamwork, leadership, and social awareness.

Educational Visits to Yakult Factory and SISFEC During My Foundation Year

During my foundation year in Agricultural Science, I had the opportunity to visit both the Yakult factory and the Sultan Idris Shah Forestry Education Centre (SISFEC) in Puchong. At the Yakult factory, I gained firsthand insight into the production of probiotic drinks, observing processes from fermentation to packaging, and understanding the importance of hygiene and quality control in food manufacturing.

The visit to SISFEC, located within the Ayer Hitam Forest Reserve, offered an immersive experience in sustainable forestry practices and environmental conservation. Engaging in activities such as jungle trekking and biodiversity studies, I developed a deeper appreciation for Malaysia's rich natural heritage and the significance of sustainable resource management. These experiences not only enhanced my academic understanding but also fostered a greater awareness of the interconnectedness between agriculture, industry, and environmental stewardship.
